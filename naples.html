Napoli

Fried dough, Caravaggio, Maradona and the loudest, most alive city in Italy. Everything below is personal opinion from repeated visits — no sponsored anything.

Last updated: July 2026 · Opening hours change constantly in Naples — always check the Maps link before heading out.

Good to know

Getting around

Metro: Line 1 (the useful one — Toledo, Dante, Museo…), Line 6 (Municipio ↔ Fuorigrotta via Chiaia/Mergellina, opened 2024) and four funiculars, all run by ANM. Line 2 is run by Trenitalia and needs its own tickets.

Line 6 finally runs longer since March 2026: Mon–Fri until about 21:00, weekends mornings only (until ~15:00).

Circumvesuviana & co. (for Pompeii, Herculaneum, Sorrento) are run by EAV and leave mostly from the lower level to the right underneath Napoli Centrale — it's confusing, plan a few extra minutes.

Artecard

Pays off quickly, even if the system is a bit confusing. Buy online / via app, you get a QR code. Urban transport is included in the 3-day versions. Going to Pompeii? Take a Campania version instead of the Naples-only one.

Neighbourhoods

  • Centro Storico — the ancient core, churches, chaos
  • Quartieri Spagnoli — Maradona, laundry, life
  • Vomero — up the hill, elegant, great views
  • Chiaia — polished, wine bars, seafront
  • Sanità — catacombs, pastries, most underrated
  • Posillipo — villas and swimming spots
  • Port / Centrale — arrival, sfogliatelle, pizza fritta
